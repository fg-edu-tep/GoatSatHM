Why We Exist

Satellites are frozen in time.
We fix that.

Every microsatellite launched today becomes an expensive, immutable object — incapable of adaptation. GOATSat changes the equation.

Status Quo — Broken

One sensor fails. Replace the whole spacecraft.

  • 🛰️
    Replacing a 200 kg satellite costs $30–100M when a single subsystem fails prematurely.
  • 🗑️
    Every discarded satellite becomes orbital debris, threatening LEO sustainability for all operators.
  • 🔒
    Hardware is frozen at launch day. No upgrades, no iteration — software-defined radios and new sensors require entirely new buses.
  • 📈
    Microsatellite CAGR is ~20% through 2030. Wasted capital compounds at that same rate.
The GOATSat Era — Modular & Dynamic

Upgrade the payload. Keep the bus.

  • 🔌
    A failed sensor? Dock a replacement module. Pay for the component, not the spacecraft.
  • ♻️
    Stranded modules remain passively docked — no debris risk. New modules restore full functionality.
  • 🔄
    SpaceFibre + CAN-FD standard interface means any certified module works with any GOATSat-compatible host.
  • 🌍
    One universal standard drives an ecosystem. Network effects compound — every adopter increases value for all others.
Technical Standard

Three layers.
One universal interface.

GOATSat v0.1 defines mechanical, electrical, and software contracts for every modular interaction in orbit.

⚙️

Mechanical Layer

Standardized docking collar with funnel-guided blind-mate geometry, keying features to prevent inverted coupling, and defined retention force specs — engineered for the structural tolerances of 10–100 kg microsatellites.

Electrical Layer

Hot-plug capable, 6-group pin sequencing (GND → PRESENT → VCC_IL → CAN → POWER → SpaceFibre) with e-fuse inrush control and hardware fault protection.

Business Architecture

We don't build the trucks.
We write the rules.

01
Revenue Stream 1

Adopter Licensing Fees

Satellite manufacturers pay an annual license fee to integrate the GOATSat interface into their next-generation bus designs and use the GOATSat certification mark in marketing. The mark becomes a signal of interoperability — a competitive differentiator in procurement.

Benchmark: HDMI Licensing Administrator charges $5–10k/yr per manufacturer. We apply a 5× aerospace premium — justified by the complexity of space-qualified interfaces and the lack of alternatives.

02
Revenue Stream 2

Module Royalties

Third-party vendors who manufacture and sell GOATSat-compatible upgrade modules pay a royalty on each unit sold. This is the dominant long-run revenue stream — high-margin, recurring, and self-reinforcing as the ecosystem grows.

Benchmark: Industry royalty range is 3–7% for aerospace IP. GOATSat applies a conservative 5.0% on an average module price of $450k — well below the GomSpace and Endurosat maturity pricing.

Revenue Stream 3

Certification Fees

One-time fees charged to test and validate that host satellites and third-party modules comply with GOATSat v0.1 and subsequent standard revisions. Benchmarked against AS9100 and DO-178C analogues in aerospace certification markets.

Conservative stance: Early adopters may receive discounted or waived certification to drive adoption. Revenue from this stream is intentionally conservative in the model.
